# Manchester United and Soccer Culture in England

## Project Overview

This site is a small digital exhibit produced for a class project for Social Movements & Sports with Professor Michel at Oakland University. This project explores the relationships between intertwined subjects: the broader culture of soccer (football) in England, and the history, identity, and global reach of Manchester United Football Club. The two halves are meant to be read together: Manchester United is impossible to understand without the country and city that shaped it, and English soccer is hard to discuss without a club like United at the table.

We treat the topic the way a museum exhibit would: short, focused sections, careful sourcing, and a layout that invites browsing as much as reading.

## How to Use This Site

Use the navigation at the top of every page to move between the four parts of the exhibit. On longer pages, an "On this page" panel on the right (or above the article on small screens) jumps directly to a section. Every factual claim is meant to be backed by a source listed on the **Sources** page; if you find one that is not, please open an issue on the project repository.

The site is fully static. It works without JavaScript, without a backend, and without any third-party fonts or analytics.

## Project Sections

- **Soccer in England** — the social, political, economic, and media context that makes English football what it is.
- **History of Manchester United** — founding, pivotal moments, key players, stadium history, supporter culture, and the club's place in global soccer.
- **Sources** — citations, references, and image credits for everything else on the site.
